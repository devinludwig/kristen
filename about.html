Kristen Diederich is a poet, self-taught painter and author of poetry chapbooks fields (2019), memento (2020), Dog Years (2022). She holds a B.A. from Portland State University and received the KIDD Tutorial Poetry Scholarship from the University of Oregon. Diederich is a graduate of the Independent Publishing Resource Centerâ€™s Poetry Certificate program, where she was the Poetry editor for 1001 Journal in 2019. Diederich has exhibited in Portland at LoLo Pass gallery, The Allery, Tartuca, Albina Press and more. A permanent collection of her paintings are housed at Wilder bar in NE Portland. Diederich is a co-host and organizer of Surprise, Surprise: A Monthly Grab Bag of Portland Poets and their poems can be found online at Soft Surface, PomPom Lit, Art and About PDX and in print at Minto Press. Community Poem (2022-23), a yearlong collaboration between Diederich, Isabel Zacharias, and Portland poets, is forthcoming from Buckman Journal. She is the creator of ALTAR, a pop-up, bringing new value to the intersections of arts and cocktails. A selection of her current work can be found at Friends of Friends at Carnation Contemporary starting July 2023.
